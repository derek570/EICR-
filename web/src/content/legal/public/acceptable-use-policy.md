# Acceptable Use Policy

This policy applies to everyone who uses the CertMate service — the iOS app, the certmate.uk web application, and any related back-end services. It is incorporated by reference into the [Beta Tester Agreement](./beta-tester-agreement.md) and forms part of the terms on which CertMate is provided.

By using CertMate you agree not to do — or attempt to do — any of the things listed below.

## 1. Service-level prohibitions

You will not:

- Use the service in any way that violates UK law or that violates the laws of any jurisdiction where you carry out an inspection.
- Use the service to produce a certificate for an inspection you are not qualified to carry out, or for an installation you have not actually inspected.
- Issue a certificate generated by the service without personally reviewing and confirming every field — the service is a dictation aid, not the authority on what the certificate says.
- Use the service in a way that interferes with another user's use of the service or that degrades its availability.
- Attempt to bypass any rate-limit, authentication, authorisation, or other access control built into the service.
- Reverse-engineer, decompile, or extract the source of the service except to the extent expressly permitted by law.
- Use automated tools (bots, scripts, scrapers) to interact with the service beyond the standard iOS or web client behaviour, unless you have explicit written permission.
- Resell, sublicense, or otherwise commercially distribute access to the service.
- Probe, scan, or test the security of the service except through a co-ordinated disclosure agreement with CertMate (contact `security@certmate.uk` before testing).

## 2. Data prohibitions

You will not:

- Enter into CertMate any personal data about an individual who is not connected to the inspection you are currently completing. Specifically, you will not dictate one customer's details during another customer's inspection.
- Enter into CertMate any data about a minor (a person under 18) unless they are unavoidably named in inspection paperwork passed to you.
- Enter into CertMate any data you have obtained unlawfully or that you do not have a lawful basis to process.
- Use the service to harass, intimidate, or otherwise harm any other person, including by recording covertly without delivering the [door script](./door-script.md).
- Use the service to record any conversation where you have been asked not to record. If a homeowner or other person present asks you not to record audio, you will not record audio for that inspection — readings will be entered manually.

## 3. Account-level prohibitions

You will not:

- Create a CertMate account using false information about yourself or about any company you act for.
- Share your login credentials with another person. Each inspector who needs access must have their own account.
- Use another person's CertMate account.
- Allow anyone other than the named account-holder to operate the iOS app or the web app while signed in to your account.

## 4. Content prohibitions

You will not upload to CertMate any content that:

- Is unlawful, defamatory, obscene, or threatening.
- Infringes any third party's intellectual property rights.
- Contains malware, viruses, or any other code designed to disrupt or compromise other users' devices.

## 5. Reporting violations

If you believe another user is violating this policy, or that the service is being abused, please contact `security@certmate.uk`. Provide as much detail as you can.

If you believe a homeowner's data has been mishandled — by you, by another inspector, or by CertMate itself — please contact `privacy@certmate.uk` immediately so that the [Incident Response Runbook](./incident-response-runbook.md) can be activated and any required notifications made within the 72-hour ICO window.

## 6. Consequences of violation

CertMate may, at its discretion:

- Suspend or terminate your access to the service, with or without notice depending on the severity of the violation.
- Notify relevant regulators or scheme providers if the violation affects regulatory obligations (for example, NICEIC scheme rules).
- Notify law enforcement if the violation involves suspected criminal conduct.
- Recover any costs reasonably incurred in investigating and responding to the violation.

The right to terminate under this policy is in addition to any other rights of termination under the [Beta Tester Agreement](./beta-tester-agreement.md).

## 7. Changes to this policy

CertMate may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be notified to subscribing inspector-customers in writing at least 28 days before they take effect, in line with the variation mechanism in the [Beta Tester Agreement](./beta-tester-agreement.md) Section 17.2.
