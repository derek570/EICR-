# CertMate Beta Tester Agreement

> **This is a draft.** It is the single highest-risk document in the compliance stack because it allocates liability between CertMate and the inspector-customer, and includes the UK GDPR Article 28 Data Processing Agreement. A fixed-fee review by a UK SaaS-specialist solicitor (~£300–£500) is recommended before the first inspector signs.

## 1. Parties

This agreement is between:

**(1) Beckley Electrical Ltd**, a company registered in England and Wales (company number **11816656**), registered office **1 MacArthur Close, Tilehurst, Reading, RG30 4XW**, trading as **CertMate** ("**CertMate**", "**we**", "**us**", "**our**"); and

**(2) the individual or company named on the CertMate account at signup** ("**You**", "**Your**", "**Inspector**"), who has accepted this agreement by ticking the "I agree" checkbox at first login.

By accepting this agreement You confirm that, if You are signing on behalf of a company or partnership, You have authority to bind that entity.

## 2. Background

(A) CertMate operates a software service that helps qualified UK electricians produce EICR and EIC electrical inspection certificates more efficiently, by capturing voice dictation, transcribing it, and using artificial intelligence to extract structured certificate fields.

(B) The service is currently in **closed beta**. You have been invited to join the beta to test the service in real working conditions and to provide feedback. This agreement governs that beta participation.

(C) Where You use the service to record data about Your own customers (typically homeowners or property occupiers), You act as the **Controller** of that data and CertMate acts as the **Processor**, in each case as defined in the UK GDPR. The terms in Section 9 (Data Processing) reflect that relationship.

## 3. Definitions

In this agreement:

- **"UK GDPR"** means the United Kingdom General Data Protection Regulation as it applies in the UK from time to time.
- **"DPA 2018"** means the Data Protection Act 2018.
- **"Data Protection Laws"** means UK GDPR, DPA 2018, the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR), and any other UK legislation relating to the processing of personal data.
- **"Personal Data"**, **"Processing"**, **"Controller"**, **"Processor"**, **"Data Subject"** have the meanings given in the UK GDPR.
- **"Customer Personal Data"** means Personal Data about Your customers (typically homeowners) and any third parties incidentally captured during inspections, which You process using the service and for which You act as Controller.
- **"Sub-processor"** means a third party engaged by CertMate to process Customer Personal Data on Your behalf, as listed at **certmate.uk/legal/sub-processors**.
- **"Service"** means the CertMate iOS app, the certmate.uk web application, and all related back-end services.
- **"Beta Period"** means the period from the Effective Date until the date CertMate ceases to designate the Service as a beta release.
- **"Documentation"** means the user-facing documentation made available at certmate.uk and within the Service, including the [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md), the [Cookie Policy](./cookie-policy.md), the [Sub-Processor List](./sub-processors.md), and the [Door Script](./door-script.md).

## 4. The Service

4.1 During the Beta Period CertMate grants You a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable licence to use the Service in the United Kingdom for the production of EICR and EIC certificates in the course of Your business as a qualified UK electrician.

4.2 The Service is provided **on an "as is" basis**, without warranty of any kind, express or implied. We make no commitment to availability, accuracy, or fitness for any particular purpose during the Beta Period. The Service may contain bugs, missing features, or material changes between sessions; You acknowledge that this is the nature of a beta.

4.3 **Inspector review is required.** No certificate produced using the Service is to be issued to a customer until You have personally reviewed every extracted field and confirmed it. You remain the qualified inspector responsible for the substantive accuracy and validity of every certificate You issue. The Service is a dictation and data-extraction aid, not the authority on the contents of a certificate.

4.4 We may modify, suspend, or discontinue any feature of the Service during the Beta Period without notice. Where the modification materially changes how Customer Personal Data is handled, Section 10 (Sub-processors) applies.

## 5. Your obligations as an inspector

5.1 You will use the Service only in connection with electrical inspections You are qualified to carry out and only for the production of EICR / EIC certificates.

5.2 You will keep Your CertMate account credentials secure. You are responsible for all activity carried out under Your account.

5.3 **You will tell each homeowner that audio dictation is taking place before recording begins.** You will use the wording in the [Door Script](./door-script.md) (or equivalent wording that conveys the same information). If a homeowner or any other person present asks You not to record, You will not record audio for that inspection and will enter readings manually.

5.4 You will not feed Customer Personal Data unrelated to a current inspection into the Service (for example, dictating an unrelated customer's details during another customer's inspection).

5.5 You will inform CertMate without undue delay if You become aware of:
- (a) any actual or suspected personal-data breach affecting Customer Personal Data;
- (b) any request received from a Data Subject seeking to exercise their rights under UK GDPR in respect of Customer Personal Data;
- (c) any complaint, query, or investigation from a regulator (including the ICO) relating to Customer Personal Data;
- (d) any change in the law applicable to Your processing that affects CertMate's obligations under this agreement.

5.6 You will comply with all Data Protection Laws applicable to You as a Controller.

5.7 During the Beta Period You will provide reasonable feedback when invited, and You will not publicly disclose unreleased features or known bugs without our prior written consent. (This restriction does not prevent You from discussing the Service in general terms, or from complying with any legal obligation to disclose.)

## 9. Data processing (the Article 28 DPA)

9.1 **Roles.** Where CertMate processes Customer Personal Data using the Service, **You are the Controller and CertMate is the Processor.**

9.2 **Subject matter, duration, nature, purpose, type of Personal Data, and categories of Data Subjects** are set out in **Schedule 1** (Description of Processing).

9.3 **Processing on documented instructions.** CertMate will process Customer Personal Data only on Your documented instructions, including with regard to transfers of Customer Personal Data outside the UK. Your acceptance of this agreement, together with Your subsequent use of the Service, is Your standing instruction to CertMate to carry out the processing described in **Schedule 1**.

9.4 **Confidentiality of processing.** CertMate will ensure that any person it authorises to process Customer Personal Data is bound by an appropriate confidentiality obligation.

9.5 **Security.** CertMate will implement and maintain the technical and organisational measures set out in **Schedule 3**.

9.6 **Sub-processors.** CertMate's use of Sub-processors is governed by Section 10 below.

9.7 **Assistance with Data Subject rights.** Taking into account the nature of the processing, CertMate will assist You by appropriate technical and organisational measures, in so far as this is possible, in fulfilling Your obligation to respond to Data Subject requests under UK GDPR Articles 15–22.

9.8 **Assistance with other obligations.** CertMate will assist You in ensuring compliance with Your obligations under UK GDPR Articles 32–36 (security, breach notification, Data Protection Impact Assessment, prior consultation), taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to CertMate.

9.9 **Breach notification (Processor → Controller).** CertMate will notify You **without undue delay, and in any event within twenty-four (24) hours**, after becoming aware of any personal-data breach affecting Customer Personal Data. The notification will include (to the extent then known): the nature of the breach, categories and approximate numbers of Data Subjects and records affected, likely consequences, measures taken or proposed to address it, and the contact point at CertMate for further information. Updates will follow as further information becomes available.

9.10 **Deletion or return of data on termination.** At the end of the provision of services relating to processing, CertMate will, at Your choice, delete or return all Customer Personal Data and delete existing copies, unless a UK legal obligation requires storage. See Section 18 (Termination) for the operational procedure.

9.11 **Audit rights.** CertMate will make available to You all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations in this Section 9 and will allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by You or another auditor mandated by You. Audits are subject to the following reasonable conditions:
- (a) audits may be carried out no more than once per year except after a personal-data breach;
- (b) audits require thirty (30) days' prior written notice;
- (c) Your auditor must be professionally qualified and bound by appropriate confidentiality terms;
- (d) audits must be conducted during normal business hours and must not disrupt CertMate's operations;
- (e) You will bear the cost of the audit unless the audit identifies a material breach by CertMate, in which case CertMate will bear reasonable costs;
- (f) where CertMate has obtained an independent third-party audit report (for example, Cyber Essentials, ISO 27001, or SOC 2 Type II) within the previous twelve (12) months, providing that report will be deemed to satisfy the audit obligation.

## Schedule 3 — Technical and Organisational Measures

CertMate implements at least the following measures:

**Confidentiality**
- All data in transit encrypted using TLS / HTTPS / WSS.
- All data at rest in cloud storage encrypted with AWS-managed keys (SSE-S3 for object storage, RDS encryption for relational data).
- API keys for Sub-processors and JWT signing secret stored in AWS Secrets Manager, never in source code or environment variables hard-coded in deployment artefacts.
- JWT bearer tokens on iOS held in the system Keychain (encrypted, device-bound).
- User-scoped storage prefixes: every customer's data lives under an inspector-ID-scoped key prefix; no cross-tenant read path exists in application logic.
- Personal-data fields automatically replaced with `[REDACTED]` in all operational logs at the Winston format-chain level.

**Integrity**
- Production deployments via signed CI pipeline (GitHub Actions).
- Code review prior to merge for material changes.
- Audit trail of every change to job data in the `job_versions` table.
- Sub-processor traffic over TLS only.

**Availability and resilience**
- Cloud infrastructure (AWS) auto-scaling and multi-AZ deployment.
- RDS automated point-in-time recovery (7-day window).
- S3 versioning enabled for object-level rollback.

**Personnel**
- Access to production data is restricted to authorised personnel using multi-factor authentication.
- Persons authorised to process Customer Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.

**Sub-processor management**
- Each Sub-processor under a written DPA imposing obligations no less onerous than those in this agreement.
- Sub-processor List maintained and published.

**Breach response**
- Documented incident response runbook (compliance Phase 5).
- Breach notification to Controller within 24 hours of awareness (Section 9.9).

**Retention and deletion**
- Retention schedule documented in CertMate's Retention Policy.
- Deletion-on-termination procedure documented in Section 14.4.

**Audit and compliance**
- Annual review of this Schedule.
- Records of Processing Activities (UK GDPR Art. 30) maintained.
- Data Protection Impact Assessment for high-risk processing (voice + AI extraction) maintained.

## Review notes for the solicitor

> **Items the reviewing solicitor is specifically asked to opine on:**
>
> 1. **Liability cap in Section 12.2** — £1,000 floor during beta, scaling to 12 months' fees in paid period. Is this defensible for a service producing regulated safety certificates? Should the cap be higher for breaches of Section 9 (data protection) specifically?
> 2. **Indemnity asymmetry in Sections 12.5–12.6.** Inspector indemnifies for misuse + cert-content inaccuracy; CertMate indemnifies for DPA breach + IP infringement. Is this balanced for the risk allocation, or does the Inspector indemnity look one-sided?
> 3. **Sub-processor change mechanics in Section 10.3–10.4.** Is 28 days' notice + right-to-terminate enough? Standard market practice ranges 14–30 days.
> 4. **Termination + data return in Section 14.4(b).** Inspector's choice of return vs delete within 30 days of termination — does this conflict with the 7-year NICEIC retention or is the exception in 14.4(b) sufficient?
> 5. **Audit rights in Section 9.11.** Are the conditions (annual cap, third-party audit acceptance, etc.) defensible or will sophisticated Inspector-customers push back?
> 6. **Definition of "material breach"** is not pinned in Section 14.3. Should it be?
> 7. **General compliance with UK GDPR Art. 28(3)** — does the DPA section satisfy every Art. 28(3)(a)-(h) requirement? A direct cross-check is requested.
> 8. **Beta-to-paid transition in Section 7.2.** Is the 30 days' notice + "separate Subscription Agreement" structure sound?
